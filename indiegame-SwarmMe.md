# Swarm Me: Outsmart the Horde in a Brutal Survival Arena!

Swarm Me is a fast-paced, minimalist survival game that flips the script on traditional action titles. Instead of fighting back with weapons, players must rely purely on movement, strategy, and environmental traps to survive an ever-growing swarm of deadly drones. It’s a game built on tension, precision, and the constant pressure of being hunted.

---

## A Survival Experience Without Weapons

Unlike most horde survival games, Swarm Me removes direct combat entirely. There are no guns, no attacks - only your ability to outmaneuver the swarm. Players must “kite” enemies, carefully guiding them through the arena and baiting them into traps to survive. This design shifts the focus from offense to pure strategy and positioning.

## Relentless Escalation and Pressure

Every second matters. As time progresses, the swarm grows larger, faster, and more aggressive, turning the arena into a chaotic test of endurance. What starts as manageable quickly becomes overwhelming, forcing players to adapt constantly and think several steps ahead. Survival isn’t about defeating enemies - it’s about lasting as long as possible.

![Game example photo 3](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/BetaONEIO/gamefolioblogs/main/assets/indiegame-assets/indiegame_swarmme/swarmme_2.png)

---

## Weaponizing the Environment

Instead of traditional combat, the arena itself becomes your weapon. Players can lure enemies into deadly traps such as fire walls, electric fences, and turrets, using them strategically to thin out the swarm. Knowing when and where to trigger these defenses is key to staying alive.

![Game example photo 4](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/BetaONEIO/gamefolioblogs/main/assets/indiegame-assets/indiegame_swarmme/swarmme_3.png)

---

## Upgrades, Buffs, and Risk Management

Throughout each run, players can collect upgrades, buffs, and special items that help tilt the odds in their favor. However, these often come with trade-offs, introducing risk-reward decisions that shape each attempt. Managing these choices effectively is crucial for long-term survival.
